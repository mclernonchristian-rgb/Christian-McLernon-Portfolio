The Brief

Make Barclays the bank graduates choose.

The task was to reposition a 335-year-old institution for a generation that has never written a cheque. Graduates are the most commercially valuable acquisition window in retail banking — they’re forming lifelong financial habits, credit histories, and mortgage potential. Win them now, keep them for life.

Objective
  • 10,000 new HEA customers
  • Six-month campaign window
  • Measurable, traceable switch
Budget
  • £500K total
  • Fully integrated: online + offline
  • ~£50 forecast CPA
Audience
  • 1.04M UK graduates/year
  • Over 18, UK-based
  • Graduated within 3 years
Market Problem

Graduates aren’t anti-bank. They’re anti-friction.

45% of UK students bypass traditional accounts entirely, going straight to Monzo, Chase, or Revolut. 89% of Gen Z prefer app-first banking — onboarding speed and design now outrank brand heritage as the deciding factor for switching. And with the Current Account Switch Service removing all friction, loyalty has to be earned every single day.

The win-condition wasn’t features — Barclays already had the better overdraft, the switching incentive, the cashback. It was relevance. A brand that spoke the same cultural language as a 23-year-old navigating their first Direct Debit.

The Insight

From product features to financial empathy.

After immersing in graduate Reddit threads, Money Saving Expert forums, and cost-of-living data, one tension kept surfacing: “You’ll find your feet.” But the feet are very expensive. “You’ll figure it out.” But everything wants a direct debit.

The strategic implication was clear: Barclays shouldn’t sell the dream. It should fund the reality. Move the audience from “a bank for my parents” to “a bank for my life now” by reframing product features as practical, real-world support.

  • Problem — Graduates feel emotionally and financially stretched. Banks read as cold and irrelevant.
  • Proposition — Barclays understands the reality of post-uni life — and rewards it.
  • Behaviour — Reappraise → Compare → Click → Start switch → Complete switch.
The Big Idea

A visual system. Not just a campaign.

Receipt of Reality — a creative platform that turns the unspoken pressures of post-uni life into a single, instantly recognisable visual: a receipt that keeps printing. Every execution is a different line item on that receipt, scaling modularly across every channel.

Voice: dry, observational, self-aware. Wry — not whinging. Confident — not corporate. Palette: warm tactile cream + receipt paper + Barclays cyan. Honest, never glossy.

Endline
  • “Reality adds up. So does Barclays.”
  • Three words on hero print
  • Earns attention at any format
Visual
  • Bird’s-eye still life of adult life
  • Rent receipts, commute cards
  • Literal till receipt as hero
Format
  • Modular: one idea, infinite executions
  • Scales across film, OOH, social, audio
  • Each tile is a standalone pressure point
Execution

A film. A tool. A wider campaign world.

Hero film “Checkout” (60s + cutdowns): a recent graduate places the objects of post-uni life on a supermarket conveyor belt — rent demand, train ticket, meal deal, work shirt, wedding invite, student-loan letter. The cashier scans each one. Deadpan. The receipt prints, and prints, and prints.

  • Receipt of Reality Generator — a web tool that turns real spending into a shareable receipt. Data captured, opt-in. Built for virality and CRM seeding.
  • DOOH “scrolling till” — train-station and uni-town billboards that print live receipts during rush hour, anchored to graduation timing and Freshers’ week.
  • Creator-led duets — money and lifestyle creators react to their own “receipt of reality,” seeded across TikTok and Instagram Reels.
  • Podcast reads — commute-window audio scripts across The News Agents, ShxtsnGigs, and Saving Grace. Formats: 6s, 10s, 15s cutdowns.
Media Plan

Find them where they already are.

Awareness
  • DOOH in uni cities + commuter zones
  • TikTok + Instagram Reels
  • YouTube pre-roll
  • Spotify + podcast reads
Consideration
  • Creator partnerships (micro)
  • Receipt of Reality web tool
  • Editorial content + earned PR
  • Paid social cutdowns
Conversion
  • Paid search: brand + competitor
  • Retargeting on landing page
  • CRM nurture sequence
  • On-app referral incentive

Priority cities: London, Manchester, Birmingham, Leeds, Bristol, Glasgow, Edinburgh — covering 62% of UK graduates. Campaign window: June–November, capturing the full graduate transition from ceremony through first Q4 of adult life.

Forecast Outcomes

Acquired today. Profitable tomorrow.

  • 10,000 completed account switches
  • 8M+ total campaign impressions
  • 160K landing page sessions
  • 20K+ application starts
  • Forecast CPA: ~£50 per acquired customer

10,000 graduates × ~£1,800 average banking lifetime value (FCA, 2024) = £18M+ lifetime return. Cross-sell to credit cards, savings, ISAs and mortgages compounds that number further. The campaign pays for itself in the first 18 months.
